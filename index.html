    • Department of ECE, UIUC

      Student Intern

      During that summer internship, I swiftly captured the relevant knowledge and concepts and became the principal investigator of my own research about that field. Briefly, I aim to use ultrasound wave to measure the attenuation coefficient of the human organs and tissues. We use a well-calibrated phantom (tissue mimicking phantom (TMP)) to acquire a reference power spectrum to cancel out the system-dependent effects, say, diffraction, time gain compensation (TGC), (socalled phantom-based method). I implemented an algorithm named SLD (Spectral Logarithm Difference) based on the SD (Spectral Difference) method. I compared two methods for estimating the attenuation coefficient from human cervix and published an abstract as the first author to a conference, Acoustical Society of America, in Los Angeles.
